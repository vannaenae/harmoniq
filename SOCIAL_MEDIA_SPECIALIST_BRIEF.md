# Social Media Specialist Brief — Harmoniq

**Role:** Social Media Specialist (Pre-Launch)
**Product:** Harmoniq — choir & worship team management app
**Phase:** Pre-launch awareness & community building
**Reporting to:** Founder

---

## About Harmoniq

Harmoniq is a web and mobile app built specifically for **worship teams and church choirs**. It replaces the chaos of group chats, spreadsheets, and scattered emails with one clean, purpose-built tool.

**Tagline:** *Vocal excellence, coordinated.*

**Core features shipping at launch:**

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Team-wide snapshot — upcoming services, attendance trends, announcements |
| **Song Library** | Shared repertoire with notes, keys, arrangements, and service history |
| **Members** | Roster management, roles, voice parts, contact info |
| **Availability** | Members flag when they can/can't make rehearsal or Sunday |
| **Attendance** | Track who showed, who was absent, spot patterns |
| **Services** | Plan setlists, assign roles, attach notes per service |
| **Announcements** | Broadcast to the whole team or specific sections |
| **Messages** | Direct and group messaging within the team |
| **Notifications** | Push alerts for rehearsal changes, new songs, service assignments |
| **Onboarding** | Invite link flow — members join in seconds, no account setup friction |

**Who it's for:**
- Worship directors and choir directors at churches of any size
- Section leaders who manage sub-groups (sopranos, altos, etc.)
- Church administrators managing multiple ministries
- Choir members who are tired of missing updates buried in WhatsApp groups

**Differentiator:** Every other team tool (Slack, WhatsApp, Planning Center, Asana) was built for generic teams. Harmoniq is built around the rhythms of a worship team — service schedules, song keys, voice parts, weekly rehearsals, Sunday setlists. It speaks the language.

## Your Mission

Build an engaged, loyal audience **before** the app launches. The goal is not follower counts — it's **a waitlist of real worship directors and choir leaders** who are excited to be first in when the doors open.

You do this by:
1. **Understanding the community deeply** (research phase)
2. **Showing up where they already are** with genuine, useful content
3. **Building Harmoniq's identity** as the brand that *gets* worship teams
4. **Converting interest into waitlist signups** over time

## Phase 1: Research (Weeks 1–3)

Before posting anything, you need to understand the community from the inside out. This is your most important phase. Do not skip it.

### Reddit Research

Spend serious time on the following subreddits. Read threads, note recurring pain points, save exact quotes (with links) from real people:

- **r/Christianity** — general Christian community, worship discussions surface here
- **r/churchmusic** — choir directors, worship musicians, praise team coordinators
- **r/worship** — worship leaders, setlist planning, team dynamics
- **r/Baptist**, **r/Reformed**, **r/Catholicism**, **r/blackchurch** — denominational communities often have very specific choir culture
- **r/musicproduction**, **r/singing** — adjacent communities where overlap exists

**What to look for and document:**

- What do worship leaders complain about most? (coordination, no-shows, last-minute changes, volunteers not knowing songs)
- What tools are they currently using, and what's broken about them?
- What language do they use? (Do they say "rehearsal" or "practice"? "Song" or "selection"? "Choir" or "praise team"?)
- What moments cause the most stress? (Easter/Christmas prep, new member onboarding, director transitions)
- What does success look like to them?
- Any threads about apps or tools they've tried — what worked, what didn't?

**Deliverable:** A research doc with 20–30 verbatim quotes organized by theme. This becomes the foundation for all content.

### Facebook Group Research

Search for and lurk in:
- "Church Choir Directors" groups
- "Worship Leaders Network"
- "Gospel Choir Directors"
- Black church music ministry groups (often very active)
- Denomination-specific worship groups

Same mission: listen first. Note the vocabulary, the pain, the wins they celebrate.

### YouTube Research

Search: "how to manage a church choir", "worship team coordination", "choir director tips", "worship director tools"

Note:
- What questions do people ask in the comments?
- Which creators serve this audience? (potential future collab targets)
- What content performs well — tutorials, vlogs, behind the scenes?

### Competitor Audit

Document how these products show up on social media and what their community says:

- **Planning Center** (the main incumbent — church management but not choir-specific)
- **Choir Genius**
- **ChoirMate**
- **Breeze** (church management)
- **Slack / WhatsApp** (what teams use by default)

For each: what do their social accounts post? What do users complain about in reviews (App Store, Google Play, Reddit)? Where is the gap?

**Deliverable:** A competitor social audit — 1 page per competitor covering their positioning, content strategy, and community sentiment.

## Phase 2: Platform Strategy

### Primary Platforms (start here)

**Instagram**
The visual and aspirational layer. Show the beauty and discipline of choir culture. Worship teams have strong visual identity — robes, Sunday morning energy, rehearsal spaces, moments of unity.

Content mix:
- Reels: short, resonant, shareable (behind the scenes of Sunday prep, "things every choir director knows", relatable humor)
- Carousels: educational ("5 signs your choir coordination is broken and how to fix it")
- Stories: polls, questions, quick tips, countdowns
- Static: quotes from directors, aesthetic choir photography

Posting cadence: 4–5x per week (2–3 Reels, 1–2 carousels, daily Stories)

**TikTok**
Raw, real, and fast. Worship team content does well here — choir bloopers, Sunday morning chaos, "a day in the life of a choir director". The algorithm rewards niche consistency.

Content mix:
- "POV: you're a choir director and it's 7am Sunday" style content
- Relatable skits about choir coordination problems
- Quick tips (under 60 seconds)
- Trends adapted to the worship context

Posting cadence: 1x daily, minimum 5x per week

**Facebook**
Where the actual decision-makers (church administrators, 40+ choir directors) spend their time. Groups especially.

Strategy: Don't just post on the Harmoniq page. Participate authentically in existing groups. Share useful content, answer questions, be a resource — not a promoter. The page itself should mirror Instagram content adapted for Facebook's format.

### Secondary Platforms (build later, months 2–3)

**YouTube Shorts** — repurpose TikTok/Reels content
**Pinterest** — choir aesthetic, worship planning templates, visual tips
**LinkedIn** — reach church administrators and ministry leaders in a professional context

### Platforms to skip (for now)
Twitter/X — low worship leader density, high noise
Snapchat — wrong demographic

## Phase 3: Content Pillars

Every post should fit into one of these four pillars:

### 1. Pain & Validation
Content that makes the audience feel *seen*. These posts go viral because they're true.

Examples:
- "The group chat where 12 people are 'read' and no one confirms they're coming to rehearsal"
- "Finding out a section leader changed the harmony two days before Sunday"
- "New member who learned the wrong arrangement because no one sent the right one"
- "Director: can everyone be there 30 minutes early? Choir: *shows up 10 minutes late*"

Source your material from the Reddit/Facebook research. Real quotes, real situations.

### 2. Education & Tips
Useful content that builds authority. Positions Harmoniq as a brand run by people who understand choir culture deeply.

Examples:
- "How to run a 90-minute choir rehearsal that actually ends on time"
- "The difference between a worship team and a performance choir (and why it matters for how you manage them)"
- "How to handle a no-show soloist the Sunday before Easter"
- "Building your choir's repertoire: how to balance new songs with muscle-memory staples"

### 3. Inspiration & Culture
Celebrating the beauty and significance of what worship teams do. Emotional, aspirational content.

Examples:
- Highlighting moments of musical excellence in worship
- Stories of choirs that transformed a church's atmosphere
- The spiritual weight of what it means to lead people in worship
- "Why the choir director is one of the most underappreciated roles in ministry"

### 4. Product Teasers (use sparingly — max 20% of content)
Glimpses of what Harmoniq does — but framed around the problem it solves, not the feature itself.

Rules:
- Never lead with "our app does X"
- Always lead with the pain, then show the solution
- Show UI sparingly and only when it's genuinely beautiful/clear
- Use "something we've been building..." language — intrigue over promotion

Examples:
- "What if your whole choir knew the setlist, the keys, and who's singing what — before they arrived on Sunday? We've been working on something." [product teaser]
- A before/after post: "Before Harmoniq: 47 unread WhatsApp messages. After Harmoniq: [clean dashboard screenshot]"

## Tone & Voice

Harmoniq's voice is:
- **Warm but sharp** — like a director who genuinely loves their choir but doesn't sugarcoat the chaos
- **Knowledgeable** — speaks the language of worship teams fluently (voice parts, setlists, service order, Sunday morning)
- **Culturally aware** — Black church choir culture is central to gospel music and worship culture; represent it authentically and respectfully
- **Not corporate** — no marketing speak, no "solutions", no "leverage"

Write like a worship director talking to another worship director — insider language, real situations, zero condescension.

## Metrics That Matter

**Phase 1 (Pre-launch):**
- Waitlist signups (primary)
- Saves and shares (signals resonance, not just reach)
- Comment quality (are real choir directors engaging?)
- DMs from potential users

**Vanity metrics to deprioritize:**
- Raw follower count
- Likes
- Impressions

A post with 200 saves from choir directors is worth more than 10,000 likes from people who'll never use the app.
